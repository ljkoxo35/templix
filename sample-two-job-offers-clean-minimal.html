The 10/10/10 Rule

When you face a difficult decision, ask yourself three questions — each framed in a different time horizon. The answers often point in the same direction.

10 MINUTES — Immediate Reaction

How will I feel about this right now?

10 MONTHS — Near Future

What consequences will emerge over the next 10 months?

10 YEARS — Long View

How will this decision shape my life 10 years from now?

Case Study: Two Competing Offers

Alex received two offers. Offer A promised a higher salary and stock options but required 60-hour weeks and frequent travel. Offer B offered a lower base salary but came with flexible hours, a clear promotion path, and mentorship.

Using the 10/10/10 rule, Alex asked:

In 10 minutes: I'd feel excited about the money from Offer A, but anxious about the schedule.

In 10 months: Offer B's mentorship and growth trajectory would likely outpace Offer A's higher starting salary.

In 10 years: The skills and network from Offer B compound — money follows mastery. Alex chose Offer B.
